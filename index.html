Limewash

Limewash, also known as lime paint, is a centuries-old surface treatment derived from natural limestone. Unlike conventional paint, limewash isn’t a typical coating; it’s a mineral finish. Authentic Limewash is a mixture of hydrated lime with water and mineral-based pigments. It is applied to porous surfaces like brick, stucco, and raw wood. Once it dries, it rehydrates and cures back to a limestone-like material with a soft, mottled, and matte appearance. Limewash is more eco-friendly than many oil-based paints and it doesn’t rely on chemical-releasing plastic resins. Use Limewash to add rustic charm to walls, furniture, or other surfaces. Limewash offers texture, depth, and a unique aesthetic.
